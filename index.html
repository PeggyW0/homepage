About Me

I am currently interning in Department MakerWorld at Bumbu Lab as a Mechanical Design Intern. I received my MPhil degree in Computer and Information Engineering at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (Shenzhen), China. During my master’s studies, I conducted research on modular self-reconfiguring robots in the Freeform Robotics under the supervision of Prof. Tin Lun LAM.

I received my bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Design, Manufacturing, and Automation from Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen), China, where I completed my undergraduate thesis under the supervision of Prof. Ren Weihong, focusing on multi-object tracking.

I am currently seeking PhD positions in mechatronics and robotics.

Research

SnailBot

SnailBot is a bionic snail-inspired robot. This version of SnailBot adopts an adaptive spherical gripper structure to enhance inter-module connections and employs a rigid magnetic track as the locomotion mechanism, which also provides a fundamental guarantee for reliable connections between SnailBot modules.

Projects

Adaptive Spherical Gripper

A gripper mechanism capable of freeform connections on spherical surfaces. Designed for modular self-reconfigurable robots to enable robust and flexible connectivity.

SnailBot Magnetic Track Locomotion

Development of a rigid magnetic track driving system to enhance locomotion stability and connection reliability between robot modules.
